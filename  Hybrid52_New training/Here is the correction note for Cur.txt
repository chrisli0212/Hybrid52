Best framing
Your model should optimize for:

directional correctness,

calibration of confidence,

robustness across regimes,

and graceful abstention when data quality is weak.

That means the 7 agents should not all be weighted equally. The most reliable agents are the ones built on real, consistently populated SPX option-chain fields such as Greeks, IV, OI, spread, bid/ask size, and chain structure, while any agent relying on sparse or synthetic trade-flow proxies should be treated as lower trust until remapped to actual available fields.

Objective to optimize
For this kind of model, do not optimize only raw accuracy. Optimize:

high-confidence accuracy,

F1 or balanced accuracy by regime,

calibration error,

and abstain rate when data completeness is poor.
​

In plain terms: a model that predicts less often but is right more often is better for your use case than a model that always gives a signal. For SPX directional prediction, reliability beats activity.
